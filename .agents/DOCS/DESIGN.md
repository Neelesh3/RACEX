# RACEX Design Bible

> "An interactive digital motorsport experience where engineering, motion and storytelling come together to create something people remember."

---

# Purpose

This document defines the visual, experiential and interaction design philosophy of RACEX.

It answers one question:

**How should RACEX feel?**

It does **not** define implementation details.

Those belong in ARCHITECTURE.md and SKILLS.md.

---

# Vision

RACEX is not a Formula One information website.

It is an immersive digital experience inspired by Formula One.

Every interaction should feel intentional.

Every transition should feel cinematic.

Every section should leave an impression.

The experience always comes first.

Information supports the experience.

# Design Philosophy

## Experience Over Features

Never add a feature because it is technically possible.

Before adding anything ask:

- Does it improve the experience?
- Does it strengthen storytelling?
- Does it make RACEX more memorable?

If not,

do not build it.

---

## Premium Simplicity

Luxury comes from restraint.

Prefer:

- large spacing
- minimal interfaces
- confident typography
- clean composition

Avoid unnecessary decoration.

---

## Curated Information

Every piece of information must earn its place.

If something can be understood visually,

do not explain it.

Avoid information overload.

# Typography

Typography should feel confident.

Large headlines.

Minimal paragraphs.

Strong hierarchy.

Readable spacing.

Avoid excessive font weights.

Avoid unnecessary uppercase.

---

# Layout

Think Apple.

Think automotive brands.

Not dashboards.

Every section should breathe.

Whitespace is a design element.

---

# Motion Philosophy

Motion is communication.

Never decoration.

Motion exists to:

- guide attention
- explain hierarchy
- reveal information
- reinforce interaction

Every animation must have purpose.

---

# Motion Hierarchy

Priority:

1. Camera

2. Lighting

3. 3D Objects

4. Interface

5. Decorative Effects

If two animations compete,

the higher priority wins.

---

# Camera Language

The camera behaves like a cinematographer.

Never like a security camera.

Rules:

- Never snap
- Always ease
- Preserve momentum
- Slow deceleration
- Natural movement
- Deliberate framing

Camera movement should feel physical.

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Lighting tells the story.

Supported environments:

- Studio
- Garage
- Night
- Sunset

Lighting should evolve slowly.

Light reveals.

Darkness creates anticipation.

---

# Material Philosophy

Materials should feel believable.

Prefer:

- Carbon Fiber
- Matte Paint
- Titanium
- Brushed Aluminum
- Rubber
- Glass

Avoid unrealistic materials.

Realism creates immersion.

---

# Interaction Language

Interactions should feel physical.

Hover

→ anticipation

Click

→ confirmation

Drag

→ resistance

Scroll

→ progression

Users should feel like they are interacting with an object,

not a webpage.

---

# Audio Philosophy

Audio supports immersion.

Never dominates it.

Allowed:

- Engine ignition
- Engine idle
- Button click
- Transition sweep
- Ambient garage

Optional:

- Background soundtrack
- Spatial audio

Audio should always be optional.

---

# Accessibility

Premium experiences are accessible.

Support:

- prefers-reduced-motion
- keyboard navigation
- readable contrast
- visible focus states

Accessibility is part of quality.

# The Reveal

## Scene 01

Darkness

A nearly black screen.

Ambient garage atmosphere begins.

RACEX logo fades in.

---

## Scene 02

Ignition

A subtle engine ignition.

A narrow light sweep reveals fragments of the car.

No full reveal.

---

## Scene 03

Discovery

The camera slowly descends.

Lighting gradually exposes the silhouette.

Movement remains calm.

---

## Scene 04

Reveal

The car becomes fully visible.

Camera settles.

Headline appears.

Primary CTA appears.

Nothing else competes for attention.

---

## Scene 05

Transition

Scroll naturally transitions into the rest of the experience.

The hero compresses gracefully.

The user should never feel they left one page for another.

# Anti-Patterns

Never build:

- Dashboard-style layouts
- Overloaded pages
- Excessive text
- Random animations
- Flashy effects without purpose
- Constant motion
- Cluttered interfaces
- Gimmicks

---

# Definition of Great Design

A design is complete when:

- It feels effortless.
- It feels premium.
- Motion has purpose.
- Information is curated.
- The interface disappears.
- The experience remains memorable.

If removing an element improves the experience,

remove it.
